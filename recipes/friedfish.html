Boca Chica Style Fried Fish

Pescado a la Boca Chica (Boca Chica Style Fried Fish)

This is a very famous dish in Dominican Republic specially if you from the city. This dish is part of our culture, the name come from the place where the fish is caught and made. Since it's catch and fried in the same place. This beach is called Boca Chica that it translate to (small mouth), it's very known in the country. For the attraction and also the food and adventure that you'll have there.

The most amazing thing about this dish is not buying it to take it home, is eating it right there in the same place where the catch it. The place, the people, the weather, the love this locals put to making this dish a memory for you to remember and everything give that dish flavor. There are places here in NY where I live now that make this dish but this amazing fried fish but nothing like home made nothing like Boca Chica Fried fish. But never the less if you want to make it home I'll add the ingredients at the bottom.

Ingredients to make this delicious dish
Type of fish to choose Ingredients you need
Red snapper 4 Cloves of garlic
Yellow tail snapper 1 tsp Dominican ground oregano
Sea bass 1 - 2 Lime
Grouper 1/2 Cup seasoning (Adobo)
Trout 1/2 tsp Salt
1/2 tsp Pepper
1/2 cup All purpose flour
1 - 2 cups Frying oil

(NOTE: Fell free to add more of this ingredients, depending on the side of the fish, this is the ingredients for a fish that is about 2 pounds.)

  1. Choose the fish

    Pick your favorite fish to try with this dish.

  2. Prepare the Seasoning mixes

    Smash or blend garlic clove. Mix in the garlic, salt, Dominican oregano, and the black pepper.

  3. mix the coating

    Now take the a separate bowl or container and mix in the flour with a little of the adobo.

    (NOTE: This is optional) You can add some extra ingredients if you like, to this mix. Add a little of pepper, paprika and you favorite spice for more color and taste.

  4. Season the fish

    Now take the fish clean and dry and make a couple of diagonal cuts to it, do this for both sides of the fish. After you have this cuts done now take the 1st seasoning the one with the garlic and put it all over the fish from the inside out. Make sure to put some in those diagonal cuts you made early.

    After making sure that theres seasoning everywhere in the fish from the inside out, now take the 2nd coating, the one with the flour and put it all over the fish as well. make sure that you can't see the skin of the fish with the flour and adobo all over the fish. (Note: Don't over do this put enough flour seasoning but don't over seasoning it will lose the flavor if you do.)

  5. Fried the Fish

    Okay almost done, keep up with me. Alright in a pot place some oil. Now wait until the oil is hot not too hot but hot like you will fried an egg.

    (Note: Be careful! Hot oil can be dangerous. If you don't feel safe frying, just turn off the stove and try grilling the fish instead, or air frying. It will still taste great, and it's a safer option.)

    Slowly place the fish inside the pot and let it cook slowly but shortly. flip it from side to side if need it to until the white flour start to get yellow keep flipping it from side to side when is nice and it's smell like is cook, Don't let it burn now take it out. Place it in a separate bowl with paper towel on it that will suck up all the extra oil on the fish.

  6. Enjoy your dish

    All done now you can serve this plate alone or with fried Plantain, Yuca, French frieds or even salad. I'll link some of my favorite sides to eat this with.
